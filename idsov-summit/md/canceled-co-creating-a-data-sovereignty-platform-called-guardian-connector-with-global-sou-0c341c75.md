# (CANCELED) Co-creating a data sovereignty platform called Guardian Connector with global south Indigenous community organizations

## Abstract
Together with a number of Indigenous community organizations across the global south, technical partners, and the US-based foundation Nia Tero, Conservation Metrics is co-creating Guardian Connector, a free and open-source platform that is designed to enable organizations to securely centralize, manage, and interpret their environmental and cultural data on infrastructure they own and control. It integrates with widely used data collection tools — including KoboToolbox, ODK, CoMapeo, Superset, Terrastories, and Timelapse — while providing a flexible, interoperable, extensible but secure architecture to accommodate diverse and evolving needs. In this presentation, I would like to share how we are trying to ground Guardian Connector in principles of data sovereignty, how it is being used by Indigenous community organizations in the Solomon Islands, Brazil, Kenya, Suriname, and Guyana, and in general, how we are trying to better understand what digital infrastructure for Indigenous data should look like. This presentation will relate to Day 1 Theme 2: Authority to Control (and also Day 2 Theme 4: Infrastructure, although that day is open to Indigenous participants only).
