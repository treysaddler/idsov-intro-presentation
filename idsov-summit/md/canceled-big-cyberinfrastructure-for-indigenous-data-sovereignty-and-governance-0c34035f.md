## Abstract
Big cyberinfrastructure in the context of this session is institutional, tribal, international and similar processes to provide digital services for indigenous data sovereignty and governance. This includes physical systems such as computers, data centers, and energy sources and software for cloud computing, research data management systems, and preprint servers among others. What services exist for indigenous peoples today, or should exist? What would your community like to build? This roundtable seeks to bring people together to discuss experiences and wishes for big cyberinfrastructure.
