## Abstract
Longstanding federal practices and policies have contributed to significant challenges in understanding Indigenous communities in Texas, deepening social, health, and economic disparities. Autonomous, community-driven efforts are needed to more accurately represent Texas Indigenous lineal descendant communities, improve access to services, and inform current and future initiatives. This session highlights two distinct but complementary approaches to achieving these goals. Through iterative cycles of planning, co-design, and reflection, both projects are guided by Advisory Committees composed of regional and cultural knowledge holders, and center the needs and priorities of Tribal communities in Texas, especially those not federally or state recognized. This session will begin with a brief overview of Find Your Story: Texas Indigenous Data Sovereignty Study, completed in 2024. This project sought to better understand Indigenous peoples in present-day Texas and Northern Mexico, culminating with a public-facing final report designed to support Indigenous wellbeing and belonging. A deeper focus will be given to a second initiative launched in 2025, which aims to strengthen relationships and mobilize resources in support of Texas Tribal communities. Rooted in trust, reciprocity, and sovereignty, this ongoing project will address longstanding gaps in visibility, access, and representation by developing a culturally relevant, trauma-informed assessment tool grounded in community-defined measures of wellbeing. These efforts contribute to a more just, accurate, and inclusive understanding of the histories and futures that shape us all.
