## Abstract
If others control your data, who controls your story? At Makwa7, we exist so Indigenous Nations can define their own story, their own governance, and their own future. Our work is dedicated to delivering sovereign, secure data solutions built with, by, and for Indigenous Peoples. This presentation will share how the Makwa7 Sovereign Digital Ecosystem from sovereign data centers located on Indigenous land to intelligent data management and sovereign GIS mapping supports communities in reclaiming, governing, and sustaining their data. We will highlight how Indigenous-designed infrastructures and tools preserve cultural knowledge, strengthen governance, and build long-term capacity for generations to come. Our approach is grounded in four pillars: • Data for Governance: Enabling Nations to collect, organize, and analyze data for informed decision-making and the repatriation of community knowledge. • Governance of Data: Embedding OCAP® principles and Indigenous-led protocols into every layer of the system, ensuring data sovereignty and protection of rights. • Infrastructure: Designing and building self-contained digital and physical systems on Indigenous land that keep information fully under community control. • Data Futures: Preparing Nations to navigate AI, digitization, and emerging technologies while remaining rooted in ancestral knowledges and practices. By showcasing practical pathways for “Coming Home: Indigenous Data Governance By Us, for Us,” Makwa7 illustrates how sovereign digital ecosystems can serve as a foundation not only for housing or departmental needs, but for entire Nations to govern their data, protect their culture, and assert their jurisdiction.
