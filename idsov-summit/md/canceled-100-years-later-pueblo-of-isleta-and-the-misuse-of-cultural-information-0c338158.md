# (CANCELED) 100 years later: Pueblo of Isleta and the misuse of cultural information

## Abstract
The Pueblo of Isleta is no stranger to cultural data extraction and misuse. In the 1920s we first began our asymmetrical relationship with Elsie Clews Parsons, who, postmortem, divulged sacred information at the later cost of an individual tribal member’s status. 100 years later, we are still haunted by this deeply harmful research methodology. The Pueblo of Isleta shares a contemporary experience regarding the misuse of our cultural data at the hands of a non-Native person. This experience demonstrates the benefits and risks of developing archives that contain culturally sensitive information. The talk will outline the types of misuse, how it took place, signs and practices of this behavior, and what we have identified to prevent this misuse. The discussion regards how the Pueblo of Isleta uses our archives to guide our decision making and strengthen our self-determination through the way we govern and provide access to our data, how it has been misused by internal staff, and how we are building infrastructure to maintain our cultural integrity. The themes of this discussion regard: Data for Governance, Governance of Data, and Infrastructure.
