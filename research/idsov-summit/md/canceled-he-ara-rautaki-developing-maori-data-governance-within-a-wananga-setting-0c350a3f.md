# (CANCELED) He Ara Rautaki: Developing Maori Data Governance within a Wananga Setting

## Abstract
This presentation explores the theory and practice behind developing a Maori data governance strategy within a wananga setting in Aotearoa New Zealand. Wananga, are Indigenous tertiary education and research institutions, and are uniquely positioned to lead in the advancement of Indigenous data sovereignty and governance in Aotearoa New Zealand. Drawing on work being done at Te Wananga o Aotearoa, the country’s largest Indigenous tertiary institution, we examine how Indigenous data governance practices are being developed and implemented in ways that are deeply embedded in Indigenous values and responsive to the aspirations of Maori communities. Unlike Western universities, wananga are strongly connected to Maori communities and grounded in Indigenous values. This enables culturally informed approaches to data governance that prioritize Maori sovereignties. We outline how Te Wananga o Aotearoa is working to support Maori data governance not only within its own institutional structures but also through collaborative research relationships and projects with universities and other community entities. We present that wananga offer a transformative approach for Indigenous data governance - one that centres Indigenous values, supports community-led decision-making, and resists the extractive tendencies of mainstream research institutions. By sharing insights from this re-search project, we contribute to broader conversations about Indigenous data sovereignty and governance and offer a vision for how Indigenous educational and research institutions can lead the way in reclaiming data for collective wellbeing and self-determination.
