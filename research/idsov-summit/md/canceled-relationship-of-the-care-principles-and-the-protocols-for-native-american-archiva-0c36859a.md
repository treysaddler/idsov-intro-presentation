## Abstract
This session will show the relationship between the CARE Principles for Indigenous Data Governance (CARE Principles) and the Protocols for Native American Archival Materials (PNAAM). Although created within different movements/decades, each was a step forward for Indigenous rights. This session will illustrate the connections between each as well as how both are founded on relationship building. The relationship between the CARE Principles and PNAAM will be situated within a larger dissertation project, leveraging the positionality and relationship of the main researcher and collaborators in a multitude of disciplines. Finally, this session will show how a non-Indigenous ally can use their experience within both Indigenous Data Sovereignty and archives to promote Indigenous Peoples’ rights to their data, utilizing Indian boarding school data as a case study.
