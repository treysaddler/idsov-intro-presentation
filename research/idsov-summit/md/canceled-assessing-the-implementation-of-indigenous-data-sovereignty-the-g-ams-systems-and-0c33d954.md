## Abstract
The Canadian Institute for Health Information(CIHI) is the largest depository of secondary health information in Canada. CIHI has halted access to Indigenous data for researchers, recognizing that research involving Indigenous data requires an active relationship with the Indigenous community from which the data originated, as well as the ability for researchers to incorporate IDSov principles into their methodologies. The goal of this project was to provide a pragmatic way for CIHI to release Indigenous data to researchers, provided they respect the rights of First Nations, Inuit, and Métis communities to their Indigenous data sovereignty. The G-AMS Systems and Implementation Quality Assessment Tool measures researchers' adherence to IDSov by asking specific questions around Governance, Access, Management, and Storage of Indigenous Data, core considerations when implementing principles like CARE and OCAP(r). Come and learn how this tool assesses the implementation of Data Sovereignty, provides essential information to Indigenous communities on how their data rights are respected, and how decision-makers can release Indigenous data for Quality Improvement and Systemic Change, while honouring Indigenous rights to sovereignty over the stories and data about them
