## Abstract
This paper presents phase 1 of a multi-phase research study examining how participatory approaches to data stewardship can shift public education away from extractive data practices and toward governance models grounded in care, collective protection, and Indigenous data sovereignty. Centering Indigenous youth and families as rights-holders, the study asks how school boards and postsecondary institutions might co-create data stewardship models aligned with the CARE Principles. Phase 1 takes the form of an engaged scoping review, in which community expertise shaped research directions around a proposed institutional data merge. While complete research activities are not bound to a timeframe or scope at the moment, actions may include: Supporting Indigenous youth and staff to invite additional members and partners to the conversation Collaborative analysis of UNDRIP in relation to Article 31 and the use of secondary data Collaborative analysis of privacy policies (OCAP®-informed) within respective institutions Participatory mapping (CARE informed) and design activities where participants (1) work to understand operative concepts of CARE and OCAP principals, (2) purpose content for data stewardship frameworks, and (3) mandatory learning for secondary data users Understand spaces for ceremony within the process of co-building a data stewardship framework We welcome the opportunity to share further reflections and discuss considerations with attendees.
