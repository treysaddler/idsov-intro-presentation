# Rematriation as Resistance - Ku`ono`ono Wahine Waimanalo as the wa`a!

Junior Ballroom J Heeka

## Abstract
Ku`ono`ono Wahine Waimanalo is a Wahine-led initiative grounded in the ancestral wisdom and practices of the Waimanalo Homestead. This program reclaims the vital roles of wahine in restoring connection with `aina through rematriation, a process centered on healing, responsibility, and interconnectedness. By elevating wahine knowledge and ceremony, Ku`ono`ono Wahine Waimanalo cultivates wellness, food sovereignty, and cultural resurgence rooted in traditional relationships between wahine, ohana, `aina, and wai. Through community guidance, the initiative creates space for wahine to revitalize cultural protocols, strengthen resilience, and honor collective strength in the face of ongoing colonial challenges. Ku`ono`ono Wahine Waimanalo embodies rematriation as active resistance, reaffirming wahine authority and nurturing sacred ties necessary for regeneration and self-determination in Waimanalo and beyond.
